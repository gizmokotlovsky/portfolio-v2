01

graphics

This section brings together different graphic and web projects created over the years — logos, websites, business cards, printed materials and visual pieces made for different people, places and needs. The visual language changes from project to project, because every person, place and story brings its own rhythm. Some works are quiet and simple, others more decorative or practical — each one belongs to a different moment and a different conversation. Graphic work has been with me for a long time: first as a hobby, then slowly as part of my professional life. I learned InDesign during my studies, used GIMP for personal experiments, worked with CorelDRAW in my first design-related jobs, and later moved into Illustrator and the Adobe workflow. It was never a straight, perfect path — more a long process of collecting tools, mistakes, habits and small visual obsessions.

02

audio & video

My first film projects were made around 25 years ago on a borrowed VHS camera — school documentaries about difficult post-war memory, national identity and the fragile relationships shaped by history. Later came subtitle frames in VirtualDub, school events, sailing videos and small visual experiments. Today, through AI media and content creation, this old interest has returned in a new form: bringing hidden texts, old ideas and unfinished moods back to life. For me, video is memory, rhythm, image, text and atmosphere finding a way to move. Some texts stayed hidden for years, waiting for a form. With AI music tools, voice experiments and digital production, they began to turn into songs, spoken fragments and atmospheric pieces. The process usually starts with my own lyrics, mood and sound direction. I shape the rhythm of the text, refine the phrasing, build the prompt, generate and select the track, then continue working with stems, voice tools, mixing and mastering. Hues is built around colours, moods and emotional states. Adjacent looks at love from a less romantic distance — relationships, misunderstanding, closeness, loneliness, and the strange difficulty of being human next to another human. For me, audio gives unfinished thoughts a body: voice, noise, melody, silence.
